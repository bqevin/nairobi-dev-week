About Developer Week Nairobi

Advancing the quality of local development

Developer Week Nairobi 2016 is Nairobi’s new tech event series with industry leading companies and local startups offering intense developer workshops, keynote talks and uniquely framed networking mixers culminating with a global hackathon competition. Developer Week Nairobi puts the focus on new dev technologies aimed at advancing the quality of technology products originating from East Africa.

Moving past good enough for Africa

Good development competes on a local level. Great development is built for the world stage. Developer Week Nairobi's mission is to foster quality development originating from East Africa that is ready to scale around the world. We believe the talent is here. It's time to connect with the resources necessary to achieve greatness.

Celebrating the talent behind the success stories

It takes a diverse team to start a company and then scale. Media reports often focus on the CEO or Founder. We who code know that countless hours go into building even the most simple of applications that power today's companies. At Developer Week Nairobi, we will hear from the CTOs and lead devs that are making companies like Twitter, LaunchKit, BRCK and M-KOPA Solar a commercial success.

Developer Week Nairobi 2016 Happenings

Here is a partial list of what you can expect this year

  1. Twitter #HelloWorld Tour In Kenya

    As one of the world's most prominent social media platforms, Twitter has played an instrumental role in the communication structure of Kenyan life. For the first time, Twitter will bring a team of developers to Kenya for a cross cultural exchange in social media and culture. Hear how powerful the East African market is to Twitter and how your app can be enriched by the Twitter Platform.
    April 28

    Keynote Speakers:

    Bear Douglas, Lead, Developer Advocacy, Twitter
    Bear is a Developer Advocate at Twitter, where she leads the Fabric and GNIP Developer Relations teams to help companies of all sizes make the most of Twitter's platform. You can find her out and about at hackathons, mobile meetups, and a cappella rehearsals with @songbirds. Former @fbplatform, @strobe, and community organizer for @emberjs.

    Juliana Chahoud, Developer Advocate, Twitter
    Juliana is a Developer Advocate and a Partner Engineer at Twitter. Previously she was an iOS Developer, building different types of apps like kids games, real estate apps and sports apps. Juliana holds a Masters degree in Computer Science and you can find her on Twitter @jchahoud.

    Bindiya Shah, Head of Partnerships, Twitter
    Bindi leads Twitter in Africa, working with content companies across the continent [predominantly in South Africa, Nigeria and Kenya] to help them use Twitter to reach new audiences and communities. Prior to this, she helped build the advertising team at Twitter UK, heading up the Account Management team. She's @Bindi on Twitter.

  2. Developer Workshops

    This is the 'heart & soul' of Developer Week Nairobi.

    Becoming a world class developer takes focus, dedication and exposure to leading development processes. Our Developer Workshops will span three days (Wednesday - Friday) in the format of Keynote sessions, lightning talks and panels covering topics like web and mobile development, user thinking, culture, trends and diversity. Each session is led by industry leading CTOs, Founders, VCs and Developers from around the world. We have curated close to 30 speakers that will instruct and inspire you towards greatness. The tools you will receive during and after these workshops will enhance the level of applications you create and in turn the quality of the East African developer ecosystem.

    These sessions are geared towards the motivated developer. Come prepared to learn. 
    April 27 - 29

  3. AngelHack Nairobi Hackathon

    AngelHack is the world’s largest and most diverse global hacker community, helping to drive open innovation of tech products, platforms and brands with extraordinary smarts, scale and speed. This competition is Angelhack's first event in Sub-Saharan Africa. Hackers compete for sponsor cash, prizes and a chance to travel to Silicon Valley for the AngelHack HACKcelerator program.
    {Register now for this event!}
    April 30 - May 1

  4. Investment Panel {powered by DraperDarkFlow}

    Led by Nigerian investor Toro Orero, Managing Partner of DraperDarkFlow, (a Tim Draper backed venture fund), Developer Week Nairobi will feature a unique panel on seeking investment for the African developer. Toro will also launch a new program called SpeedUP Africa seeking the coolest startups in Africa for VC investment.
    April 24 - May 1

  5. CodinGame Live Coding Challenge

    Think you are a rockstar developer? Compete against other developers during Developer Week Nairobi '16 using a special platform that merges programming and video games for prizes.
    April 27 - 29

  6. Agile & Scrum Training

    In order to develop world class applications, programmers must integrate efficient development processes. Agile development focuses on providing maximum value to the end user. Agile teams adopt industry leading practices and focus on creating high value applications. Rarely offered in East Africa, come learn the skills to become a certified agile developer or project manager. Read this article for more info about Agile methodology.
    April 25 - 26

  7. Tech Hiring Mixer {powered by Duma Works}

    We think the best talent for local companies is local talent. So we are taking one evening of Developer Week Nairobi to connect local hiring managers with local talent. System admin, devops, data scientists, web & mobile developers even support for tech positions are welcome. Come prepared to network with hiring managers ready to talk with qualified talent!
    April 27

  8. Conference Expo

    3D printers; innovative solar solutions; on demand mobile applications; rugged mobile Wifi, these are all examples of tech solutions that you can experience first hand at the Developer Week Nairobi Expo while talking with the developers who are making the products a reality for the market.
    April 27 - 29

  9. Work Hard, Play Hard

    You didn't think we would work this hard without partying did you? Check back for some exciting news about our Friday night afterparty. Trust us, you won't want to miss this one!
